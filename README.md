The deployment of High Performance Computing (HPC) facilities driven by Artificial Intelligence (AI) and rising computing  demands across diverse regions requires a shift from traditional thermal monitoring to a multiphysics framework that accounts for atmospheric pressure, temperature, and chemical stressors. These codes support hte simulation approach that combines modeling  and monitoring data to evaluate how local environments affect hardware survival and sustainability. Analyzing different zones in Latin America  and the Caribbean, corresponding to level sea (coastal/saline), middle sites in the Andes (mid-altitude/stable), and high sites (high-altitude/low-pressure), reveals an 'Altitude-Temperature Paradox,' where thin high-altitude air accelerates hardware aging more than sea-level heat.  By linking physical degradation with $CO_2e$ impact, we show that sustainability depends on geographical balance.
